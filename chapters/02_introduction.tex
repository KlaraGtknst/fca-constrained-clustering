\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

Traditionally, knowledge-based systems struggle to model the domain of a given text corpus~\cite{cimiano_learning_2005}. 
Cimiano et Al. denote this problem ``knowledge acquisition bottleneck''.
They propose an FCA-based approach to automatically derive concept hierarchies from domain-specific textual data to structure information into categories and facilitate work with knowledge-bases.
Since many texts lack explicit definitions of terms due to basic domain-knowledge often being assumed rather than explicitly written (e.g., ``a tiger is a mammal''), Cimiano et Al. use syntactic dependencies based on the \textit{distributional hypothesis} to enrich texts with domain-knowledge in the form of an FCA word (pair) context.

Since this approach relies solely on syntactic structure present in the text corpus, the available knowledge is very limited.
Hierarchical constraints can be thought of a tool to model certain degrees of certitude with respect to the information's validity.
