\subsection{Comparison of Resulting Concept Lattices}
\label{sec:lattice_comparison}

Notably, the number of BankSearch documents considered for the two concept lattices differs. 
The topic model-based lattice includes only 10\,970 documents that do not contain erroneous text (as previously done by \cite{gutekunst2025conceptual}), whereas the ground truth lattice encompasses all 11\,000 documents together with their gold standard labels. 
This discrepancy arises from the requirement of valid textual input for the topic model to assign topics reliably, which necessitated the exclusion of documents with corrupted text data.

Furthermore, while the ground truth lattice in Figure~\ref{fig:mlb_concept_lattice} contains the complete set of concepts derived from the gold standard labels, the topic model-based lattice in Figure~\ref{fig:iceberg_concept_lattice} is constructed as an iceberg lattice. 
Consequently, it includes only those concepts whose intents satisfy a minimum support threshold of $0.05$, thereby excluding the most specific concepts. 
This restriction is necessary due to the size of the full topic model-based lattice, which contains more than $590$ concepts (cf. Figure~\ref{fig:concepts_support}) and would therefore be impractical to visualize and interpret in its entirety.

In Figure~\ref{fig:mlb_concept_lattice}, the ground truth lattice attributes are denoted by $M_i$, which results from the script constructing the formal context based on the \ac{mlb} constraints. 
In total, 14 attributes are present. 
Among those, only two attributes, $M3$ and $M4$, directly correspond to the topics, i.e., \texttt{Biology} and \texttt{Astronomy}, respectively. 
The remaining ground truth topics are represented as intersections of attributes. 
For example, \texttt{Sport} corresponds to $M0 \cap M1$, while its original child topics \texttt{Soccer} and \texttt{Motor Racing} correspond to the attribute combinations $M1 \cap M2$ and $M0 \cap M2$, respectively. 
Overall, the original ground truth topics (except for $M3$ and $M4$) are located on the lowest level directly above the bottom node of the lattice.

The topic model-based lattice shown in Figure~\ref{fig:iceberg_concept_lattice} contains 30 concepts with support of at least $0.05$. 
Concepts are labeled by the topic IDs contained in their intents. 
In contrast to the \ac{mlb}-informed concept lattice, the original topics are not explicitly represented as individual concepts in this lattice.

Structurally, the iceberg lattice consists of only two levels besides the top node. 
Consequently, it exhibits substantially less hierarchical structure than the ground truth lattice, which is expected due to the pruning effect introduced by the iceberg constraint.

% Glckstad2013UnsupervisedKS use Jaccard for overlapping intents
To assess whether both concept lattices contain similar document clusters (i.e., overlapping concept~\cite{Glckstad2013UnsupervisedKS} extents), we compute the Jaccard similarity between all pairs of concept extents from both lattices. 
For two concept extents $A$ and $B$, the Jaccard similarity is defined as
\[
J(A,B) = \frac{|A \cap B|}{|A \cup B|}.
\]

The resulting similarities are visualized in the heatmap shown in Figure~\ref{fig:concept_lattice_similarity}. 
Most concept pairs exhibit a Jaccard similarity close to zero, indicating that their respective document clusters have little or no overlap.
Excluding the trivial similarity between the top concepts, only three concept pairs exhibit a Jaccard similarity greater than $0.6$.
The gold-label concepts from the most coherent concepts in Table~\ref{tab:concept_jaccard_similarity} are located in the middle region of the lattice (Figure~\ref{fig:mlb_concept_lattice}) and do not correspond to semantically meaningful ground truth topics. 
Overall, this analysis indicates that the two lattices exhibit very limited structural similarity, which is expected given the differences in the underlying data as well as the iceberg restriction applied to the topic model-based lattice.

\subsection{Consistency-Lattice vs. Coherence-Lattice}

The consistency-lattice (Figure~\ref{fig:consistency_lattices}) is obtained by intersecting the ground truth lattice with the topic model-based lattice as described in Section~\ref{sec:integrated_lattice_computation}. 
In this case, only the top concept is shared between the two lattices, resulting in a consistency-lattice consisting of a single concept.
In contrast, the coherence-lattice (Figure~\ref{fig:coherence_lattice}) is constructed by defining a new formal context based on intersections of concept extents whose Jaccard similarity exceeds $0.6$. 
Each attribute represents the membership of documents in the intersection of such concept extents.
