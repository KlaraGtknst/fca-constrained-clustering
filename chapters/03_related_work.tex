\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related_work}

% 1. Constrained Hierarchical Clustering -> explain MLB constraints
%% clustering with constraints
Clustering algorithms group data points based on different criteria into clusters.
Prior knowledge about the dataset can be modelled as constraints.
The simplest constraints are pairwise \textit{must-link} and \textit{cannot-link} constraints, which require objects to be in the same or different clusters, respectively.
While conventional constrained clustering avoids violating constrainst while grouping data points into clusters on a single level of granularity~\cite{wagstaff2001constrained}, constrained hierarchical clustering considers different levels of granularity~\cite{bade_hierarchical_2014}.
%% limits of pairwise constraints: hierarchical clustering
However, pairwise constraints are inherently ambiguous in hierarchical settings because objects may be together at one level of the hierarchy but separated at another.
Bade et al.~\cite{bade2006personalized,bade_creating_2008} therefore find pairwise constraints to be insufficient for hierarchical clustering, and introduce \ac{mlb} constraints into \ac{hac}.
These \ac{mlb} constraints encode the order in which objects should be merged during agglomeration, effectively capturing relative hierarchical relationships rather than flat equivalence conditions~\cite{bade2006personalized,bade_creating_2008,bade_hierarchical_2014}.
In prior work, \ac{mlb} constraints altered the similarity measure during the merge process~\cite{bade2006personalized}, delayed merging two clusters until violated constraints are resolved~\cite{zhao2010hierarchical}, or was incorperated in matrices-based approaches~\cite{zheng2011semi}.

% 2. Extracting concept hierarchies from text corpora
%% 2.1. Non-FCA approaches
Automatic concept hierarchy generation from text corpora has been approached in various ways.
Hierarchical text classification assigns documents one or more labels organized in a taxonomy~\cite{yu2022constrained}, which implicitly creates a hierarchical structure of documents based on their assigned labels. 
Approaches range from regularization-based classifiers~\cite{gopal2013recursive}, similarity-based matching of documents and labels in a common embedding space~\cite{li2019hiercon} to neural sequence models~\cite{risch2020hierarchical,yu2022constrained} and recent \ac{llm}-assisted taxonomy enrichment~\cite{zhang2025teleclass}.

%% 2.2. FCA-based approaches
Due to \ac{fca}-based concept hierarchy generation being a form of conceptual clustering on documents that yields intensional descriptions of abstract concepts via their intents (i.e., features)~\cite{cimiano_learning_2005}, research has also explored the use of \ac{fca} for concept hierarchy generation from text corpora.
%% Text corpus as formal context: We use gutekunst2025conceptual, but with LDA instead of top2vec
Prior work proposed different approaches to modeling a text corpus as a formal context by extracting features from textual data as attributes and documents as objects.
Feature extraction included bag-of-words presence of terms~\cite{cigarran_twitter_fca_2016}, \ac{lda}~\cite{wang_topic_feature_2021,wang_topic_hierarchy_2023} or other topic model-based approaches~\cite{hirth_ordinal_2024,gutekunst2025conceptual}. % hirth_ordinal_2024 uses own trained topic model refered to as SSH21, but is something trained using non-negative matrix factorization, gutekunst2025conceptual use top2vec

Constraints have been introduced to encode mutually exclusive attributes~\cite{hidayat_constrained_fca_2024},

prune the concept lattice to only contain concepts incolving intents which are considered important. %~\cite{belohlavek2006formal,belohlavek2009formal,mao_constrained_fca_2017}.
They respectively defined the notion of importance on the attributes~\cite{belohlavek2009formal}, on the closure operator~\cite{belohlavek2006formal}, or by introducing an additional operator~\cite{mao_constrained_fca_2017}.

There has also been work on extending Attribute Exploration with multiple contradicting or incomplete experts~\cite{felde2021triadic,felde2022attribute} to explore shared implication of all subsets of a group of experts.

However, prior work either incorporates hierarchical \ac{mlb} constraints into clustering or focuses on constrained concept extraction without employing \ac{mlb} constraints.
Moreover, to our knowledge, no work compares concept lattices derived from constraint-based hierarchical knowledge and probabilistic topic models.
