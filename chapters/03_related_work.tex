\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related_work}

In related work on constrained hierarchical clustering, Bade and Nürnberger (2014)~\cite{bade_hierarchical_2014} address a key limitation of traditional pairwise must-link and cannot-link constraints, which are inherently ambiguous in hierarchical settings because objects may be together at one level of the hierarchy but separated at another. 
To overcome this, they introduce \ac{mlb} constraints, a ternary constraint that explicitly encodes relative hierarchical relationships by stating that two objects must be grouped together at a more specific level of the hierarchy than either is with a third object. 
This formulation directly captures ordering information between merges and is therefore better aligned with the goal of learning full cluster hierarchies rather than flat partitions. 
The authors formally analyze the properties of \ac{mlb} constraints, including symmetry and transitivity, and propose an instance-based extension of \ac{hac} that enforces these constraints during the merge process. 
Empirical results on text clustering tasks show that incorporating \ac{mlb} constraints provides effective structural bias, improving the recovery of meaningful hierarchies while avoiding the level-selection problems associated with adapting pairwise constraints to hierarchical clustering.
