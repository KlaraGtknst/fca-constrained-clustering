\begin{abstract}
Traditional attribute exploration iteratively discovers domain information using a domain expert.
Inconsistencies between multiple experts are usually ignored by using systems of shared implications.
In this work, we analyze the difference between two different knowledge base informed concept lattices.
We employ \ac{mlb}-constrained attribute exploration and knowledge extraction based on a statistical knowledge base.
\ac{mlb} constraints are systematically extracted from a gold-label topic hierarchy and subsequently mapped to the
corresponding document corpus.
The second knowledge base, namely a \ac{lda} topic model, builds a document-topic-context based on statistical
inference of the document corpus.
The impact of both knowledge basis are compared both qualitatively (human) and quantitatively by intersecting both
contexts.

\end{abstract}
