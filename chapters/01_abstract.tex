\begin{abstract}
Traditional attribute exploration iteratively elicits domain knowledge from an expert to construct a formal context.
When multiple experts are involved, potential inconsistencies are typically disregarded by relying on systems of shared implications.

In this work, we investigate the differences between two knowledge-base-informed concept lattices.
We employ \ac{mlb}-constrained attribute exploration and knowledge extraction from a statistical corpus-based knowledge
base.
The \ac{mlb} constraints are systematically derived from a gold-label topic hierarchy and subsequently mapped onto the corresponding document corpus.
As a second knowledge base, we utilize an \ac{lda} topic model, which constructs a document–topic context through statistical inference over the same corpus.

The impact of both knowledge bases is evaluated qualitatively through human assessment and quantitatively by intersecting the resulting formal contexts.

\end{abstract}
