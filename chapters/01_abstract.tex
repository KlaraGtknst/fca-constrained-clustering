\begin{abstract}
Traditional attribute exploration iteratively elicits domain knowledge from an expert to construct a formal context.
When multiple experts contribute, inconsistencies are commonly resolved by restricting the exploration to systems of
shared implications, thereby potentially discarding conflicting knowledge.

In this work, we explicitly address such inconsistencies by investigating the structural differences between two
knowledge-base-informed concept lattices derived from the BankSearch dataset.
We employ \acs{mlb}-constrained attribute exploration and knowledge extraction from a statistically
derived corpus-based knowledge base.
The \acs{mlb} constraints are systematically obtained from a gold-label topic hierarchy and subsequently mapped onto
the corresponding document corpus.
As an alternative knowledge source, we utilize an \acs{lda} topic model, which constructs a document–topic context
through statistical inference over the same corpus.

The impact of both knowledge bases is evaluated qualitatively through human assessment and quantitatively by analyzing
the intersection of the resulting formal contexts.
While the qualitative analysis reveals substantial structural differences between the two concept lattices, their
intersection provides a principled mechanism for consolidating potentially conflicting knowledge bases.

\end{abstract}
