\begin{abstract}
We present FCA-constrained clustering, an approach to document clustering that combines \ac{fca} with topic modeling through constraint-based attribute exploration. 
Our method constructs an incidence relation between documents and \textcolor{red}{clusters} using attribute exploration, where the exploration process is guided by an expert, i.e., a set of \ac{mlb} constraints. 
These constraints are automatically extracted from an external knowledge base derived from the topic model \ac{lda}. 
Specifically, we apply \ac{lda} to assign topics to documents in the corpus, retaining only the most representative topics to ensure their relevance. 
From the resulting iceberg concept lattice of documents and their associated topics, we systematically extract MLB constraints that encode semantic relationships between topics. 
These constraints serve to prune the solution space of all possible clusterings derivable from the attribute exploration incidence relation, effectively incorporating domain knowledge from the topic model into the \textcolor{red}{clustering process} using \ac{fca}. 
Our approach demonstrates how statistical topic modeling can inform and constrain clusterings.

\end{abstract}
