\begin{abstract}
\ac{fca} makes it possible to generate a hierarchical conceptual order without needing expert ontology. In light of this advantage, the question arises
as to whether the generated order is consistent with existing knowledge. To ensure consistent concept hierarchy generation, it has already been suggested to use hierarchical
constraints, also known as MLB constraints.

In this work, we propose a method to compare and merge knowledge derived from \acs{mlb} constraints with an additional
knowledge source in the form of a hierarchy, such as a topic model.
We apply this approach to the BankSearch dataset by exploring the universe of a closure operator provided by the \acs{mlb} constraints.
These constraints are derived from a gold-label topic hierarchy and subsequently mapped onto the corresponding document corpus.
As our supplementary hierarchical knowledge source, we utilize an \ac{lda} topic model, which constructs a
document–topic context through statistical inference over the same corpus.

To understand the interplay between these sources, we examine the structural differences between the resulting
knowledge-base-informed concept lattices.
The influence of both knowledge bases is assessed qualitatively through manual review and quantitatively by analyzing
the intersection of their conceptual structure.
While the qualitative review highlights distinct structural variations between the two concept lattices, their
intersection offers a practical approach for consolidating these differing knowledge representations.

\keywords{\acs{fca} \and Constraints \and Topic Modeling \and Clustering.}
\end{abstract}
